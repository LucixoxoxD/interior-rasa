§ The Studio

A point of view
rooted in place.

We do not begin with a mood board. We begin with the land — its light, its grain, the way afternoon settles into a room differently in Delhi than in Bangalore. Rasa draws from the deep material traditions of the subcontinent: stone cut from Rajasthan, brass poured in Moradabad, linen woven in Maheshwar. These are not accents. They are the architecture of feeling.

Each project is a considered argument for how one ought to live — unhurried, sensory, specific to the person who inhabits it.

§ What We Do

Considered from the ground up.

01
Residential Design

We take on a small number of residential commissions each year, working closely with the family over the full duration of the project. From spatial planning and material specification to art curation and soft furnishings — nothing is delegated without care. Our residential work ranges from urban apartments to landmark bungalows across Delhi NCR, Mumbai, Bengaluru, and Hyderabad.

02
Farmhouse & Villa Projects

The farmhouse sits at an interesting threshold — between city life and a certain freedom from it. We approach these projects with a particular attention to land, light, and the specific leisure they afford. Stone, water, shade, texture: the vocabulary here is elemental. Projects across the Aravallis, the Konkan coast, and the Nilgiris foothills.

03
Hospitality

Select hospitality commissions — boutique hotels, private clubs, and owner-operated restaurants — where the brief rewards a genuine interior point of view. We are not production designers. We are brought in when the owner understands that atmosphere cannot be assembled from a catalogue.

04
Bespoke Furniture & Styling

Every significant project produces a handful of pieces made to no standard dimension. We work with craftspeople — woodworkers in Saharanpur, blacksmiths in Rajkot, weavers on the Deccan plateau — to realise furniture, lighting, and objects that exist nowhere else. Available as a standalone service for those furnishing a completed space.

§ How We Work

A deliberate process, not a production line.

Every commission moves through four phases. The pace is unhurried by design — we have found that the quality of a finished interior is directly proportional to the quality of time given to each stage. Most residential projects run 12 to 24 months from first meeting to handover.

01 Discovery
Briefing Site visits, client conversations
Space Analysis Orientation, light, proportion
Programme Room-by-room requirements
02 Concept
Material Direction Palette, texture, craft vocabulary
Moodboard Presented for alignment
Spatial Narrative How the rooms speak to each other
03 Design
Design Development Drawings, elevations, detail studies
Material Sourcing Artisan visits, sample approvals
Furniture Specification Bespoke commissions placed
04 Realisation
Site Supervision Weekly reviews, quality checks
Styling & Dressing Art, objects, textiles placed
Handover The house becomes a home
§ Material Intelligence

The hand that made it
is still in the room.

India has one of the most sophisticated craft traditions in the world. We treat this not as heritage to be preserved behind glass but as a living material vocabulary — one that improves every surface it touches and carries within it a quality of attention that manufactured goods cannot replicate.
