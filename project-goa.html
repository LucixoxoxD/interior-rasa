The Goa
Retreat

Villa · Goa 2022
LocationAssagao, North Goa
TypePrivate Villa
Built-up Area6,100 sq ft

CompletedDecember 2022
§ The Brief

A house that makes the silence feel designed.

The site is a colonial-era Portuguese house in Assagao, set within a mature cashew orchard. The clients — a Mumbai family seeking a retreat from the city — did not want renovation in any typical sense. They wanted the house to feel as though it had always been theirs, without the signs of effort.

We preserved the original laterite stone walls and terracotta tile roof. New interventions — a pool deck in Kota stone, a library pavilion at the garden edge, a screened dining terrace — were designed to read as extensions of the colonial logic rather than additions to it.

The interiors draw on the Goan vernacular: oyster-shell windows refitted in teak, lime-washed walls in a shade drawn from the soil of the site, furniture in caned rattan and reclaimed teak. The house is designed to be opened entirely to the garden by 6 AM and closed to afternoon sun by 1 PM — a rhythm the family has made part of daily life.

§ Materials Used

The land gave us everything we needed.

Laterite Stone
On-site, Goa — Walls & Boundaries

The original colonial structure. Retained in full, repointed with lime mortar. Gains a deep ochre patina through the monsoon season.

Kota Stone
Rajasthan — Pool Deck & Terrace

Honed finish for the pool deck; a rougher bush-hammered surface for the garden paths. Both in the dark grey-green variety.

Natural Rattan & Cane
Sourced Goa — All Furniture

All seating and case furniture in hand-woven cane on teak frames. Made by a workshop in Mapusa that has operated since the 1960s.

Lime Wash, Site-Pigmented
Applied on-site — All Interior Walls

Three rounds of lime wash, the final coat tinted with raw ochre sourced from a nearby laterite quarry. No two walls read exactly the same.
