Rasa Design StudioNew Delhi, 2026
§ The Studio

A place,
a practice,
a point of view.

Rasa was founded in New Delhi in 2008 by Tara Mehrotra, with the conviction that Indian interiors deserved better than either faithful heritage reproduction or unrooted modernism.

Eighteen years later, the studio operates from Mehrauli with a team of twelve — architects, material researchers, and craftspeople in close collaboration. We work Pan-India and on select international commissions for clients who understand that a home of consequence takes time.

§ Philosophy

We do not decorate. We make arguments about how to live.

Every project at Rasa begins with a question that is not about aesthetics: how does this family move through their day? What do they hear in the morning? What do they want to feel when they return from work? The answers to these questions are the brief. The material and spatial decisions that follow are attempts to answer them precisely.

We work with Indian craft not as a gesture toward culture but because it is the most intelligent response to the Indian climate, the Indian light, and the Indian relationship to domestic life. A Rajasthani jali is not decorative — it is a ventilation strategy and a light-diffuser that no manufactured product equals. A handwoven durrie is not sentimental — it is washable, repairable, and improves with age.

Our interiors age well. We design them to. The patina of use — the brass that darkens, the lime plaster that breathes, the teak that acquires a history — is not an accident. It is the entire point. A house is not a showroom. It is a record of a life being lived.

§ The Team

Small by design.

Tara Mehrotra
Founder & Principal Designer

Trained at SPA New Delhi and the Architectural Association, London. Returned to practice in India in 2006 after two years with Adjaye Associates. Founded Rasa in 2008. Her work has been exhibited at the India Design Forum and the Kochi Biennale.

Ishaan Rao
Design Director

Architect and interior designer from Bengaluru. Specialises in material research and the documentation of Indian craft traditions. Has spent six months in Kutch, documenting resist-dye textile processes. Leads all hospitality projects.

Priya Subramaniam
Senior Designer — South India

Based in Bengaluru. Manages all projects in South India with particular expertise in the Dravidian vernacular — Chettinad stone, Athangudi tile, and the spatial logic of the traditional Brahmin agraharam. Trained at CEPT Ahmedabad.

§ Practice History

Eighteen years, one direction.

2008

Studio founded in Mehrauli

Tara Mehrotra establishes Rasa from a studio in the Mehrauli Archaeological Park, with two staff and a single residential commission in Defence Colony.

2012

First farmhouse commission — Aravallis

Completion of a weekend farmhouse near Manesar marks the studio's first significant departure from urban residential work. The project is featured in AD India.

2015

South India expansion; Bengaluru office

Priya Subramaniam joins as lead for South India. The studio takes on its first Bengaluru residential project — a garden house in Whitefield — and an ongoing relationship with Karnataka clients begins.

2018

First hospitality commission — Chambers at Lodhi

A private members' dining room in New Delhi's Lodhi neighbourhood. The project earns the studio a mention in the Financial Times's 'How to Spend It' supplement.

2023

House on Aurangzeb Road — AD India cover

The studio's most significant residential project to date, published across six pages in the March 2023 issue of Architectural Digest India. A milestone that reaffirms the studio's editorial presence.
