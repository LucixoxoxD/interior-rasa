§ The Brief

The sea is outside. The interior needed to hold its own.

The apartment occupies an upper floor of a tower in Bandra West, with unobstructed views of the Arabian Sea to the west. The temptation in such a space is to subordinate everything to the view — to make the interior a frame. We did the opposite.

The clients — a couple with an art collection assembled over fifteen years of travel — wanted a home that could stand in conversation with the sea, not simply defer to it. Rooms with character. A living room that earns the right to be turned away from the window.

We introduced deep terracotta plaster walls in the study, a custom blackened brass library wall, and flooring in reclaimed teak with a matte finish. The art collection — including two large Atul Dodiya works and a commissioned textile by a Kutch weaver — was integrated into the spatial sequence from the beginning, not added at the end.

§ Materials Used

Sea salt and terracotta.

Reclaimed Burmese Teak
Sourced Mumbai — Flooring

Salvaged teak from a colonial-era building in Fort. Matte oiled finish. Gains character with each monsoon season.

Terracotta Plaster
Applied on-site — Study & Dining

A warm ochre-red pigmented lime plaster, applied in two coats and sealed with beeswax. Deepens slightly in the evening light.

Blackened Brass
Moradabad — Library Wall System

Custom shelf brackets and library ladders in oxidised brass. A material that is simultaneously industrial and precious.

Kutch Woven Textile
Gujarat — Commission, Bedroom

A large-format commission from a weaver in Bhuj. Natural indigo and madder dyes on cotton warp. Will take years to fully settle.
