§ The Brief

A Lutyens bungalow that remembers what it is.

The house had been through three renovations before it came to us — each one layering Eurocentric finishes over bones that were always, quietly, Indian. The brief from the family was deceptively simple: make it feel like theirs.

We began by stripping back: removing dropped ceilings that had swallowed the original proportions, exposing lime-plastered arches, letting the stone floors breathe again. What remained was a house with an instinct for shade, for cross-ventilation, for the pleasure of a room that dims gracefully into afternoon. We worked with that instinct rather than against it.

The material palette was drawn from within a day's drive of the house: Dholpur sandstone from Rajasthan, brass fittings cast in Moradabad, handwoven durries from Panipat, and a suite of bespoke furniture made in collaboration with a craftsman in Saharanpur whose family has worked in sheesham for four generations.

§ Materials Used

A palette drawn from proximity.

Dholpur Sandstone
Rajasthan — Flooring & Cladding

A warm pink-beige stone that absorbs heat and releases it slowly through the evening. Used across ground-floor circulation.

Lime Plaster Walls
Applied on-site — All Interiors

Three-coat lime finish, burnished by hand. Breathes with the building. Colour varies subtly with humidity and angle of light.

Poured Brass Hardware
Moradabad — Fixtures & Fittings

All door handles, light fittings, and plumbing hardware cast from a single foundry. Unlacquered — will patinate over years of use.

Panipat Durries
Haryana — Rugs & Floor Textiles

Flat-woven cotton durries in a custom stripe, made to width. Washable, appropriate to the climate, and deeply Indian in character.
